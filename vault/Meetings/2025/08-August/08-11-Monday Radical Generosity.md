**Summary**

The speaker discusses the concept of radical generosity, emphasizing that God is inherently generous, as evidenced throughout the Bible. They share personal experiences of God's generosity and the transformative power of the church. The speaker then transitions to the topic of intercessory prayer, highlighting its importance and sharing several stories that illustrate its effectiveness, including historical accounts and personal anecdotes. The sermon concludes with a call to prayer for healing and for the radical provision of a new city campus.

**Detailed Summary**

*   **Radical Generosity:**
    *   **God's Generosity:** The speaker asserts that God is incredibly generous, a theme evident from Genesis to Revelation. Examples include creation for human enjoyment, providing for His people, and even granting free will. God's generosity is also seen in His persistent return to the Israelites despite their rejections, and supremely in giving His Son, Jesus, for eternal life (John 3:16).
    *   **Reflecting God's Image:** As followers of Jesus, believers are called to reflect God's generous image in their daily lives.
    *   **Personal Testimony:** The speaker shares how God saved them through Jesus and how the church transformed their life through acts of generosity, such as extending hospitality and providing housing. Before finding Jesus, the speaker struggled with addiction, debt, shame, and a criminal record, but found peace through Jesus's generosity.
    *   **Family Influence on Generosity:** The speaker poses a question about how generosity was modeled in listeners' families, noting that people often unknowingly adopt their family's approach to money and generosity. They share examples of parents who were generous but didn't discuss money, and parents who argued about money, leading to similar patterns in their children's marriages.
    *   **Kingdom vs. Scarcity Mindset:** Christians are encouraged to teach their children about generosity and money, emphasizing a "kingdom way" of giving. Two contrasting mindsets are presented: a "kingdom of abundance" where God provides all needs, and a "world of scarcity" where one must hoard resources.
    *   **Biblical Basis for Generosity:** Proverbs 11:24-25 (Message version) is cited: "The world of the generous gets larger and larger. The world of the stingy gets smaller and smaller. The one who blesses others is abundantly blessed. Those who help others are helped."
    *   **Legacy and Missions:** The church's legacy of generosity has led to growth, enabling the purchase of buildings and investment in other campuses. 10% of legacy funds go to missions, which has resulted in over a million dollars raised for various projects worldwide, including community centers, church buildings, houses, training centers, schools, orphanages, and hospitals in Mozambique, Lesotho, Cambodia, Mexico, Uganda, Ukraine, Moldova, and Ethiopia.
    *   **Planning for Generosity:** Isaiah 32:7-8 is quoted, emphasizing that "generous people plan to do what is generous." The speaker likens this to planning a date night in marriage – without a plan, it often doesn't happen. Practical examples of planned generosity include babysitting for others, lawyers offering pro bono services, and tradesmen helping with projects. Tithing is suggested as a way to test God's faithfulness in this area.

*   **Intercessory Prayer:**
    *   **Definition:** Intercessory prayer is defined as praying for other people, bringing them into God's presence, in contrast to petition, which is praying for oneself.
    *   **Biblical Examples:**
        *   **Aaron:** Aaron carrying the names of the tribes of Israel on his chest piece when entering the holy place symbolizes intercession (Exodus).
        *   **Moses:** Moses holding up his hands during battle, with Aaron and Hur supporting him, illustrates how intercession leads to victory in spiritual warfare (Exodus 17:11-13).
    *   **Stories of Intercession:**
        *   **James Matheson:** A Scotsman who interceded all night for the 93rd Highlanders during the Crimean War. Soldiers reported feeling peace and warmth from a man walking among them in the trenches, later identified as Matheson at a communion service.
        *   **Mozambique Missions:** Graham and Lucy Forsted taught missionaries to pray in the heavenlies before visiting unreached people groups. This led to instances where people, even 99% Muslim, recognized missionaries from dreams.
        *   **David Livingstone:** While in Africa, Livingstone was targeted by a tribe. He prayed, and later learned that 39 "giants" surrounded his compound, deterring the attackers. Years later, he discovered that 39 people were praying for him at a church prayer meeting on that exact date.
    *   **Importance and Impact:** Intercession invites God's power to fight on behalf of others. While results may not be immediate, continuing to pray is crucial.
    *   **Overcoming Evil:** Colossians 2:13-15 is cited, stating that through Christ's death on the cross, God forgave sins, canceled charges, and disarmed spiritual powers, publicly shaming them. This provides confidence to approach God in prayer.
    *   **Jesus as Intercessor:** Jesus is the perfect sacrifice and intercedes on our behalf, fighting our battles.

*   **Call to Prayer:**
    *   **Activation:** The speaker encourages everyone to pray, even if they've never prayed aloud before, emphasizing that simple prayers are effective.
    *   **Prayer Points for 21 Days of Prayer and Fasting:**
        *   Fresh outpouring of the Holy Spirit.
        *   Healing and freedom.
        *   Revival and alignment for the nation and missions.
        *   Radical provision for a new city campus.
    *   **Focus on Healing:** The speaker specifically calls for prayer for healing, inviting those who need it to raise their hands and encouraging others to lay hands on them and pray for supernatural healing and freedom, including for mental health battles.
    *   **Focus on New City Campus:** After praying for healing, attendees are asked to form small groups to pray for radical provision for the new city campus, visualizing its blessing to the city and praying for random giving and God's intervention.
